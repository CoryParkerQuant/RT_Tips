01

FAQ by Topic

First place to look for "how do I…?" and "why does…?"

Entries, exits & order timing

What's the difference between EntrySetup, EntryLimit, and EntryStop?

EntrySetup is a true/false condition making a symbol a candidate. EntryLimit and EntryStop are prices — not conditions. No limit/stop = market order.

What do ThisClose, NextClose, and NextOpen mean?

NextOpen: evaluated at today's close, fills tomorrow's open (most common). NextClose: fills tomorrow's close. ThisClose: acts at today's close — cannot be a live order placed in advance.

Can I use a different exit time per exit reason?

Not directly — ExitTime is a single setting. Workaround: drive a differently-timed exit through an ExitStop/ExitLimit price guaranteed to trigger, leaving other exits on the main ExitTime.

How do I build a trailing stop?

ExitStop is recalculated every bar, so any formula referencing the bar trails automatically. To prevent loosening, wrap with max(PrevExitStop, ...).

ExitStop: max(PrevExitStop, Highest(H, 10) - 3*ATR(10))
If more limit-order setups qualify than MaxPositions allows, which fill?

Use SetupScore (with MaxSetups) to rank which orders get placed, and EntryScore (with MaxPositions) to rank which fills to keep. Without scores, RealTest falls back to alphabetical — biases results.

What's the difference between ExitRule and ExitStop?

ExitRule is a true/false condition evaluated at the bar's close. ExitStop is a stop price checked against the bar's range — can catch intraday gaps through the level. Choose based on whether you want close-based or stop-price exit.

No trades are being generated — how do I debug?

Check in order: Is EntrySetup ever true? Is Side specified? Is Quantity > 0? Are MaxPositions/MaxInvested/other caps blocking? Enable full trade keeping — skipped trades appear in the trade list with a reason column.

How do I take only one new entry per day when many setups qualify?

Cap with MaxEntries: 1 and rank candidates with EntryScore so the best one is chosen.

How do I stop re-entering a stock right after it exits?

Add a cooldown to the setup using a count of recent exits — require no exits in the last N bars before allowing a new entry.

Position sizing & allocation

What are the three Allocation modes?

S.StartEquity — non-compounded, fixed base. S.Equity — compounded, each strategy on its own equity. Combined(S.Equity) — compounded, all strategies sharing one pool. Put sizing math in Quantity, not Allocation.

Should I use S.Alloc or S.Equity in my Quantity formula?

Prefer S.Alloc — updated once per day after the close, stable between entry and exit order computation. S.Equity changes as positions close during the day, causing order quantities to drift.

Common sizing recipes
// Equal weight
Quantity: 100 / MaxPositions  |  QtyType: Percent

// ATR-scaled volatility sizing
Quantity: 0.01 * S.Alloc / (3 * ATR(20))  |  QtyType: Shares

// Fixed risk to stop
Quantity: 0.01 * S.Alloc / Abs(FillPrice - stopPrice)  |  QtyType: Shares
What's the difference between MaxPositions, MaxSetups, MaxInvested, and MaxExposure?

MaxPositions: cap on concurrent holds. MaxSetups: cap on placed limit/stop orders (avoids selection bias). MaxInvested: cap on current MTM dollars. MaxExposure: cap on committed capital at entry. Use the one that matches the constraint you actually mean.

Data & imports

Simplest Norgate import
Import:
    DataSource: Norgate
    IncludeList: .Russell 3000 Current & Past
    EndDate: Latest
    SaveAs: data.rtd

Leading dot = dynamic watchlist. "Current & Past" includes delisted members — required to avoid survivorship bias.

Which adjustment mode — Capital, TotalReturn, or CapitalSpecial?

Use Adjustment: Capital for order generation and most backtesting. TotalReturn for long-horizon total-return comparisons. Never use CapitalSpecial for live order generation — it adjusts share counts for special dividends; your broker does not.

Does RealTest use adjusted or unadjusted prices?

Formulas use as-traded (unadjusted) prices and volume; indicators are adjusted as of the current bar. This removes a subtle split-related ranking bias that pre-adjusted data introduces.

What does Padding do?

Fills missing bars (holidays, halts, mismatched calendars) so every symbol aligns to a common date list. Padding: AllMarketDays is the common choice. Mismatched calendars are a frequent source of surprises when mixing FX/crypto with stocks.

Multi-strategy & rotation

How do I stop two strategies holding the same symbol?
EntrySetup: setupConditions and Combined(Shares) = 0

Also drops open positions from ranking automatically because SetupScore only ranks symbols passing EntrySetup.

How do I rotate or rebalance monthly?
firstOfMonth: Month <> Month[1]   // first bar of new month
rebalance:    EndOfMonth            // last bar of month
→EndOfMonth uses the global calendar; Month uses the symbol's own. Adding FX/crypto can shift EndOfMonth triggers — use Month <> Month[-1] for symbol-specific boundaries.
How do I hold until rank drops below a wider threshold to reduce churn?
ExitRule: rotate and posrank > worstRank  // worstRank > N

Results & metrics

What do ROR, MAR, Sharpe, Sortino, UPI mean?

ROR: annualized return (geometric when compounding on). MAR: ROR / max drawdown. Sharpe: excess return over total volatility. Sortino: excess return over downside volatility only. UPI: uses ulcer index (depth + duration of drawdown) instead of volatility. All three risk-adjusted ratios depend on the risk-free rate set.

What's a reasonable expectancy?

Forum rule of thumb: short-term systems need roughly 0.4–0.5% per trade so slippage and costs don't erase edge. Strategies trading less often need more per trade.

Mark-to-market vs closed equity — which do the stats use?

Default: mark-to-market (unrealized) equity — industry norm. Set MarkToMarket: False to base every stat on closed-trade P&L only. Open-position swings affect drawdown far more than total return.

Why did results change between two identical runs?

Almost always the data changed (Norgate overnight revision) or a setting differed (Padding, Adjustment, Compounding, date range). Pin the data file and date range for reproducible tests. Norgate revisions finish ~6:45 AM ET — generate live orders morning-of, not prior evening.

Formulas & syntax

How do I reference a previous bar?
C[1]              // yesterday's close
H[5]              // high 5 bars ago
C[BarsHeld]       // close on the entry bar
HHV(H, 50)[1]     // 50-bar high as it stood 50 bars ago (not current)
My L < LLV(L,5) setup returns no trades — why?

Today's low can't be lower than the lowest low of the last 5 bars, which includes today. Compare to the prior bar: L < LLV(L,5)[1]

Fast all-time high calculation
// Self-referential — far faster than Highest(C, BarNum)
ATH: if(BarNum = 1, C, Max(C, ATH[1]))
How do I reference another symbol's data?
spxTrend: Extern($SPY, C > MA(C, 200))   // $ = stock symbol
vixLevel: Extern($$VIX, C)                // $$ = symbol starting with $
eqCurve:  Extern(@stratName, S.Equity)    // @ = strategy
wklyVal:  Extern(~Weekly, ROC(C, 12))     // ~ = timeframe
⚠Omitting the $ prefix causes "unrecognized syntax element" error.
How do I label exits with reasons?
ExitRule: Select(C > targetPrice, "target",
                 C < stopPrice,   "stop",
                 BarsHeld >= 10,  "time")

When used in ExitRule, the Select() must be the entire rule.

Optimization & validation

How do I set up an optimization?
Parameters:
    maLen: 50 to 200 step 10 def 100

def is the value used in normal (non-optimize) runs.

Does parameter order matter for speed?

Yes. The top parameter varies fastest (innermost loop). Put slow, expensive parameters (long moving averages) first so cached calculations are reused. Order AND-conditions cheapest-first also short-circuits expensive terms.

How do I pick robust parameters?

Favor a broad plateau over a lone sharp peak. The 2D optimization heatmap makes plateaus visible — choose from the middle of one. A parameter at the edge of the tested range is a red flag the range was too narrow.

Charts & output

How do I plot an indicator on the chart?
Charts:
    ma200: MA(C, 200) {^}      // price pane
    rsi2:  RSI(2)    {^1#2}    // indicator pane 1, 2 decimal places
    rsi14: RSI(14)   {^2#2}    // indicator pane 2

{^} price pane · {^1}/{^2}/{^3} indicator panes · {#2} decimal places · {%} percent

How do I export results to CSV?
TestSettings:
    SaveStatsAs:     Results\stats_?testname?.csv
    SaveTradesAs:    Results\trades_?testname?.csv
    SavePositionsAs: Results\positions_?testname?.csv

Coding Tips & Best Practices

Idioms, structures, and performance habits used by experienced forum members

Script structure: Data, Library, Parameters

Choose the right home for each item
Parameters:
    length: 20          // constants and optimization ranges — values only, never formulas

Data:
    ma200: MA(C, 200)   // expensive, reused, per-symbol time series — store it

Library:
    spyClose: Extern($SPY, C)  // evaluated on demand, in caller's context
⚠Never put constants in Data. length: 4 in Data with 1000 stocks × 1000 bars = 1 million copies of the number 4 = 8MB wasted.
Data, Library, Parameters sections can repeat in a script

Item names must be globally unique, but you can position a section directly above the strategy that uses it. Grouping related code makes long multi-strategy scripts far easier to maintain.

Share code across scripts with Include
Include: my_indicator_library.rts

Pastes the other script's contents in memory before the test runs. Fix a shared indicator once and every strategy that includes it updates. Right-click a result → "Show Original Script" to see the fully expanded version.

Writing fast scripts

Order AND-conditions cheapest-first
// Faster: cheap price filters first, long MA last
EntrySetup: C > 10 and C < 200 and MA(V,20) > 100000 and MA(C,50) > MA(C,200)
→This same short-circuiting can hide a NaN warm-up period. Put the longest-lookback condition first if NaN propagation matters.
Pre-calculate indicators once in Data for one-pass speed
Data:
    ma200: MA(C, 200)        // one-pass: stored, O(bars)
    // ma200x: MA(C, 200) + 0  // NOT one-pass: operator outside function
    // maVar: MA(C, myLen)      // NOT one-pass if myLen varies per bar

Use #SlowCalc to force per-bar behavior when needed.

Cap SinceTrue with a length argument
barsSinceEarnings: SinceTrue(Event(1), 65)  // never look back more than 65 bars

Without the cap, if the condition hasn't happened in hundreds of bars, every bar pays for a long backward scan.

Use #OnePerDate for market-wide values
Data:
    #OnePerDate spyTrend: Extern($SPY, C > MA(C, 200))

Computes once per date instead of once per symbol. Forum users report optimizations dropping from ~7 minutes to 2 by applying this to the right items.

Formula language

Conditions need a comparison; prices must not have one
EntrySetup: C = HHV(C, 20)   // condition: has comparison
EntryStop:  HHV(C, 20)       // price: no comparison
ExitRule:   Dividend > 0     // condition — NOT just "Dividend" (floored to int)
Handle missing values with NoNan and IsNaN
trendDir: NoNan(trendDir[1], 1)             // carry forward, default 1 on bar 1
label:    if(NoNan(?Strategy = 'mr'), 1, 0)  // guard ?Strategy NaN
Pick a formula by index with Item()
Parameters:
    stopType: 1 to 3 def 1
Library:
    stop1: C - 2*ATR(20)
    stop2: Lowest(L, 20)
    stop3: FillPrice * 0.95
    stop:  Item("stop{#}", stopType)  // optimizer sweeps named alternatives
Detect crossovers with Cross()
macdUp:   Cross(MACDH(13,26,9), 0)   // histogram crosses above zero
macdDown: Cross(0, MACDH(13,26,9))   // histogram crosses below zero

Self-referential items & state

Carry a value forward until a reset — WhenTrue()
triggerOpen: WhenTrue(crossedBelow50[1], O)  // holds Open from trigger bar
Flip / ExRem-style state gates
Data:
    buy:   C = HHV(C, 10)
    sell:  C = LLV(C, 10)
    flip:  not sell and (buy or flip[1])   // on at buy, off at sell
    fresh: buy and not flip[1]              // only the first buy of a run
Fresh-setup gate to prevent re-signaling an open trade
Data:
    inpos: if(BarNum = 1 or exit[1], 0, if(inpos[1] or setup[1], 1, 0))
Strategy:
    EntrySetup: not inpos and setup
Two Data items can't reference each other's [1] values — use TestData
TestData:
    flip: if(BarNum = 1, C, if(flip[1] < 0, ...))
    pct:  if(flip[1] < 0, 5, pct[1] + 1)

TestData runs sequentially inside the backtest loop and allows forward references with offsets.

Cross-sectional # functions

Rank symbols against each other
Data:
    momRank: #rank ROC(C, 20)    // rank 1 = highest value
    mrRank:  #rank 1 / RSI(2)    // inverted: most oversold ranks highest
Rank only qualifying symbols — use NaN to exclude
offRank:  #rank if(InList("Offensive"), relMom, NaN)
bondRank: #rank if(InList("Bonds"),     relMom, NaN)

NaN drops non-candidates from rank slots entirely.

Multi-lookback momentum factor (VAA/BAA/HAA)
factor: 1/4 * (12*ROC(C,1) + 4*ROC(C,3) + 2*ROC(C,6) + 1*ROC(C,12))
Measure breadth and rank within sectors
pctAboveMA:  #sum (C > MA(C,200)) / #count 1 * 100
sectAvgRoc:  #avg #BySect ROC(C, 5)
newHighs:    #sum #ByIndu (InSPX and C = HHV(C, 252))
→Cross-sectional functions only work reliably on daily bars. Derive weekly/monthly logic from daily data using weekly_ prefix or Extern(~Weekly,...).

Other symbols & timeframes

Extern() prefix reference guide
Extern($SPY, ...)      // $ = stock symbol
Extern($$VIX, ...)     // $$ = symbol itself starting with $
Extern(&CL, ...)       // & = relative futures symbol
Extern(@stratName, ...)  // @ = strategy name
Extern(~Weekly, ...)   // ~ = timeframe
Weekly/monthly values in a daily test
Data:
    weekly_ma:   MA(C, 10)                  // computed on weekly bars
    monthlyRoc:  Extern(~Monthly, ROC(C,12)) // updates on period's final bar
Self-referential circuit breaker using a benchmark clone
Benchmark: mrTracker
    EntrySetup: universe and setup   // always trades, no caps

Strategy: MeanRev
    MaxSetups: if(Extern(@mrTracker, S.Equity < Avg(S.Equity,50)), 0, 10)

Entries, exits & stops

MaxSetups vs MaxPositions — two-stage pipeline
MaxSetups:    40                  // place the best 40 limit orders
SetupScore:   1000 - ROC(C, 5)
MaxPositions: 5                   // keep at most 5 fills
EntryScore:   1000 - ROC(C, 5)
// Don't exceed capacity:
MaxSetups:    MaxPositions - S.Positions
Anchor a stop to the entry bar
ExitStop: FillPrice - atrStretch[BarsHeld]  // BarsHeld as offset always reaches entry bar

BarsHeld = 1 the day after entry, 2 the next — so [BarsHeld] always looks back to the entry day.

Time stops and percentage targets
ExitRule:  BarsHeld >= 5                     // use >= not = so missed bars still trigger
ExitLimit: FillPrice * (1 + target/100)       // long profit target

Position sizing recipes

All four core recipes
// 1. Fixed fractional (equal weight)
Quantity: 100 / MaxPositions  |  QtyType: Percent

// 2. Volatility-adjusted (ATR)
Quantity: 0.01 * S.Alloc / (3 * ATR(20))  |  QtyType: Shares

// 3. Fixed risk to stop
Quantity: 0.01 * S.Alloc / Abs(FillPrice - stopPrice)  |  QtyType: Shares

// 4. Scale by condition/regime
Library:
    weight: if(strongSignal, 1.0, 0.5)
Quantity: weight * (1 / MaxPositions)  |  QtyType: Percent

Multi-strategy portfolios

Share one capital pool with Template + Combined
Template: common
    Allocation: Combined(S.Equity)
    QtyType: Percent

Strategy: MeanRev
    Using: common
    Quantity: mrAlloc / mrPositions
Weight strategies by regime
Library:
    bullWeight: if(bullMarket, 1.0, 0.5)
    bearWeight: if(bullMarket, 0.5, 1.0)
Strategy: LongBias
    Quantity: bullWeight * 0.1 * S.Alloc

Hidden Gems

Powerful features most users miss

Bring outside information into your backtest

EventListFile — inject earnings, macro events, any dated data
Import:
    EventListFile: earnings.csv
EntrySkip: SinceTrue(Event(1) > 0) < 5   // skip 5 bars after event
Survivorship-free universes with constituency tracking
Import:
    Constituency: $SPX, $NDX
    IncludeList: .Russell 1000 Current & Past
Strategy:
    EntrySetup: InSPX and C > MA(C, 200)  // InSPX true only on actual member dates
RTD files — import once, test all day

Importing builds a binary .rtd file. Every subsequent test loads in seconds. Think of .rtd files as saved query results, not raw data. Keep several: a deep survivorship-free history for backtests, a trimmed current-universe file for live scans.

Honest validation

Built-in walk-forward optimization

First-class feature, not hand-scripted. Re-optimizes on each in-sample window and applies parameters to the following out-of-sample window automatically. Tests whether the process of periodic re-optimization holds up, not just whether one fixed parameter set fit the past.

The optimizer is a general-purpose batch runner

With date intervals it runs one test per year, or a rolling window incremented through history, with no parameters at all. "How sensitive am I to start date?" becomes one click.

2D optimization heatmaps

Plot a metric as color against two parameters at once. A broad flat plateau is far more trustworthy than a lone sharp peak — and the heatmap makes the difference instantly visible.

Realistic account modeling

RiskFreeRateSym — make Sharpe mean something
Settings:
    RiskFreeRateSym: %FFYE

Default Sharpe uses 0% risk-free rate. Across eras of 5% cash that quietly flatters every strategy.

Model short borrow fees
Commission: baseComm + if(Side < 0 and IsExit,
    borrowPct/100/252 * Abs(FillValue) * BarsHeld, 0)
CashInOut — deposits, withdrawals, contributions
CashInOut: if(EndOfMonth, 2000, 0)   // contribute $2k each month

Richer output

Export everything to CSV
TestSettings:
    SaveStatsAs:     Results\stats_?testname?.csv
    SaveTradesAs:    Results\trades_?testname?.csv
    SavePositionsAs: Results\positions_?testname?.csv

Particularly useful when using AI to develop and test trading ideas via the RealTest command-line interface.

Bridge to live trading

Command-line automation
realtest -import my_script.rts
realtest -scan   my_script.rts

Task Scheduler updates Norgate, runs the script, produces orders — fully hands-off daily pipeline.

Meta-strategies

Volatility targeting and dynamic leverage
Library:
    vol:  Extern(@tracker, StdDev(ROC(S.Equity,1), 20) * Sqr(252))
    mult: Min(volTarget / vol, maxLev)
Strategy: vt
    Quantity: S.Equity * mult / FillPrice
DynamicSizing — rebalance in place without round-trips
Strategy: ETF1
    DynamicSizing: True
    QtyType: Percent
    Quantity: Select(EndOfMonth and Symbol = $ETF1, 20)
StatsGroup — shared kill-switch across multiple strategies
StatsGroup: sg
    Using: bench1, bench2
Strategy: live
    EntrySkip: Extern(@sg, hveq20 >= 30)

Performance

Skip slow formulas during optimization
#ifndef Optimize
    expensiveScanFormula: ...
#endif

Excludes chart/scan-only calculations from parameter sweeps at parse time.

Plot booleans to verify signals

A boolean data item plots directly as 0 or 1 — drop a condition onto the chart and see exactly which bars it fired on. Fastest way to confirm a signal before building a strategy around it.

04

Gotchas

Behaviors that look like bugs but aren't — scan here before assuming a bug

Order timing & execution

ThisClose exits can't become live orders
// Replace: exit at this close if up day
ExitRule: BarsHeld >= 5
ExitTime: NextClose
ExitLimit: C + 0.01    // fills tomorrow at close if close > today's close
Stop and target on same bar — the Ambiguity setting

When both stop and target fall inside the same daily bar's range, RealTest can't know which was hit first. Test under both Ambiguity: Stop and Ambiguity: Target. If results differ significantly, stops/targets are too tight — treat as a red flag, not a tuning problem.

Entry-day stops quietly flatter results
ExitStop: if(BarsHeld = 0, 0, yourStopFormula)  // conservative: no stop on entry bar

Tight entry-day stops produce more (smaller) trades, mechanically inflating Sharpe even when win rate drops.

Limit prices are tick-rounded before comparison

Equality tests like long_limit = L[-1] fail because the formula variable holds the unrounded value. Use LimitExtra: 0.01 instead.

Separate MOO slippage from other fills
OpenSlip: 0       // market-on-open fills
Slippage: 0.01    // all other transactions

NaN & formula evaluation

TRUE or NaN = NaN — condition order matters
skip: IsNaN(rank1) or rank1 > MaxPos   // correct — NaN guard first
// rank1 > MaxPos or IsNaN(rank1)        // WRONG — NaN propagates from first term
0 * NaN = 0, not NaN

Short-circuit optimization: when the evaluator sees 0 * expression, it skips evaluating the expression entirely and never discovers the NaN. Add an explicit IsNaN() check where the distinction matters.

Short-circuiting can hide indicator warm-up
// Put longest-lookback condition first so its NaN propagates correctly
EntrySetup: C > MA(C, 500) and InRUI and C > 5
EMA in Data vs inline EMA can diverge for long lengths

Data section EMA is one-pass from bar 1. Inline EMA approximates with ~5x lookback. For long EMAs on long histories this can flip a comparison. Pre-calculate long exponential indicators in Data and reference everywhere.

Formula context

Data / Library / Strategy evaluate at different times

Data: computed once before test runs — no strategy context, no BarsHeld, no S.Equity. Library: evaluated on demand, inherits caller's context. Strategy: full context, inside the backtest loop per bar per symbol.

Offsets on data items are not split-adjusted

Direct price references like C[1] are split-adjusted as of the current bar. Once a data item is calculated, offset references to it don't receive split adjustment. Where split-sensitivity matters, use the indicator function directly in the comparison.

Parameters persist between script runs

Intentional — parameters persist in memory until the next script with a Parameters section is run. Add an empty Parameters: section to a script to clear the previous set.

Position sizing math

Keep Allocation to three values only
Allocation: S.StartEquity      // or S.Equity, or Combined(S.Equity)
Quantity: 50                   // sizing logic lives here, not in Allocation
QtyType: Percent
Always use Abs(FillValue) in commission formulas for shorts
Commission: Min(Max(1.00, 0.005 * Shares), 0.01 * Abs(FillValue))

Short fills have negative FillValue. Without Abs(), commission becomes a credit, compounding equity into absurd territory until it crosses MaxInvested's default cap of 1.0E+14.

S.Exposure vs S.Invested vs S.Usage

S.Exposure: sum of position sizes at entry / S.Alloc — open P&L doesn't move it. S.Invested: current MTM value / S.Alloc. S.Usage: peak intraday exposure — can exceed overnight for day-trading.

Global market calendar

Adding FX or crypto makes stocks miss holiday bars
Import:
    Padding: AlignWithSymbol
    PadAlignSym: SPY

FX trades on US holidays — the holiday becomes a global market date, every stock gets a "missing bar", and RealTest won't enter right after a missing bar.

EndOfMonth uses global calendar; Month uses symbol's own
rebalance: Month <> Month[-1]   // symbol-specific — safe with mixed calendars

Adding EURUSD can shift EndOfMonth triggers for all stocks.

Data, splits & dividends

Backtesting today's index members is look-ahead bias
IncludeList: .Russell 1000 Current & Past   // always use this

Current list only contains survivors. Every bankruptcy and chronic underperformer has been filtered out by the future.

Norgate revises data overnight

Revisions finish ~6:45 AM ET. Generate live orders morning-of, not prior evening. Results changing between identical runs is almost always a data revision or a setting difference.

Capital vs TotalReturn — both include dividends, at different times

Capital: credits dividends at trade exit (realistic, use for backtesting and order generation). TotalReturn: applies dividends as share adjustments on ex-date (long-horizon total-return comparisons). Neither drops dividends.

Ranking & selection

SetupScore only needed when also using MaxSetups

SetupScore (with MaxSetups) decides which candidate orders get placed. EntryScore (with MaxPositions) decides which placed orders are assumed filled. You only need SetupScore if you're also using MaxSetups.

Cross-sectional functions only work reliably on daily bars

Run on daily bars and derive weekly/monthly logic from daily data. Weekly/monthly #rank also returns NaN when symbols have mixed missing bars at period boundaries — fix with Padding: AllMarketDays.

Charts & display

Chart bar size is independent of the script's BarSize

Chart formulas recalculate for whatever the chart is currently displaying. For consistency, compute in Data and plot the stored item.

Charts can "see" data the backtest couldn't — this is not look-ahead

Charts are drawn after the test from the complete dataset. The backtest's decisions used only prior data. To verify backtest context, plot MA(C[1], 200) not MA(C, 200).

Benchmarks always report as non-compounded

Intentional — benchmarks don't contribute to combined stats. If you need a compounded buy-and-hold comparison, define it as a Strategy instead.

Live Trading with OrderClerk

Practical workflow from first connection through daily operation and troubleshooting

Daily workflow (start here)

The four steps — in this order, every day

1. Open TWS with Trade History window showing 5–7 days of fills.
2. Connect OrderClerk to IB — pulls fills, updates OrderClerkTrades.csv.
3. Run RealTest in Orders mode against those up-to-date positions.
4. Load orders in OrderClerk, review, transmit.

⚠Cardinal rule: connect OrderClerk to IB before generating orders. Skipping causes duplicate entry orders for already-filled positions. Most "out of sync" problems trace here.

Connecting to Interactive Brokers

TWS API silently disables after updates

In TWS Global Configuration → API → Settings, enable "Allow ActiveX and Socket Clients." Check this after every TWS update — the most common cause of OrderClerk failing to connect.

Keep the Trade History window open and showing multiple days

OrderClerk reads fills from the TWS Trade History (executions) window. Closed window or "today only" = no prior-day fills received = phantom cancels. Open it, set 5–7 days, leave it open before every connect.

Use Stable Offline TWS — not Latest

The "latest" channel auto-updates without user intervention causing automation failures. Plan for IB's weekly Sunday two-factor reset — re-authenticate manually before Monday or use IBC.

Separate folders for paper vs live

The OrderClerkFolder setting tells RealTest where OrderClerk's files live. Sending live orders into a paper folder (or vice versa) corrupts position tracking. One folder per account, path set explicitly in each script.

Order types by exchange

ThisClose is incompatible with OrderClerk

Use NextClose for all close-based entries and exits. ThisClose requires knowing today's closing price before it happens — causally impossible for advance order placement.

Crossing orders — "would cross a related resting order"

When one strategy sells a symbol the same day another buys it, IB rejects the pair. Fix: stagger with Good-After-Time, or submit one as OPG and one as MOC so they don't sit live simultaneously (plain market orders — algos can't take good-after-time).

Large orders may require an algo

IB rejects very large plain market orders. Submit as IB algo order or cap order size in the sizing formula.

Reconciliation

Edit OrderClerkTrades.csv in plain text only — never Excel

Excel silently rewrites dates to local format and can add stray rows, both of which break OrderClerk. Edit in a plain-text editor, back up first, close OrderClerk before editing.

Resynchronize after a missed day

Switch OrdersMode to OrderClerkSync — replays trade list to last known point, backtests the gap, generates catch-up orders. Review carefully before transmitting — sync generates both entries and exits.

Entry showing 0 shares filled = missed fill, not cancel

Classic cause: connecting after trade date with Trade History window not showing that day. Restore trade list from before the missed date and reconnect with history window open.

Corporate actions

Quick reference

Splits: usually automatic — only intervene if share count mismatch flagged.
Takeovers: add exit row at cash-per-share, no commission; use IB corporate action report for settlement details.
Spinoffs: add as new trade at IB's cost-basis allocation price; recheck next day for revisions.
Ticker changes: update in trade list and regenerate. IB symbol and data-vendor symbol can change at different times.

Timezones & holidays

Holiday handling — simplest approach

If automation runs on a holiday, it regenerates the same orders as the prior day and OrderClerk rejects duplicates harmlessly — no action needed. The HolidayList alternative is fragile across day-before scheduling and multiple markets.

EndDate: Latest can resolve to the wrong day

If some symbols already have today's bar when orders are generated, "Latest" resolves to today instead of yesterday, dating orders wrong. Use an explicit prior-session EndDate or align the calendar.

Automation

Leave a pause between connect and order generation

In a fully automated sequence, give OrderClerk a brief pause after connecting before generating orders — fills need to fully sync first. Without it, a race can produce an exit order for a position that has already been closed.

Size to the live account balance dynamically

Let OrderClerk write the current net liquidation value to a file on each connect and have the script size from that — orders track real account value as it grows, shrinks, or receives deposits.

Multiple accounts & exchanges

One OrderClerk folder per exchange

Safest multi-exchange setup: separate folder per market, each driven by its own script at its own time. A shared folder requires markets' runs to be well separated in time and daily difference review.

Rename a strategy only when flat

OrderClerk tracks orders by strategy name. Renaming mid-trade strands the old reference and order status is lost. Rename only when no open orders, then update both script and trade list.

Troubleshooting by symptom

Quick reference

"Unable to convert server timezone" loop: transient minute-boundary mismatch — disconnect and reconnect.
WaitNextId error: TWS update disabled API — re-enable "Allow ActiveX and Socket Clients."
WaitFills code 322: fills query too broad — reduce days requested, connect daily after close.
BadDate / 0001-01-01: missing Date field in order template or clock timezone mismatch.
Error 200: symbol not found or exchange mapping wrong.
Error 201: stale/illiquid — generate orders during market hours.
Too large: submit as IB algo order or cap size in Quantity formula.
Stuck pending orders: cancel via OrderClerk open-orders view; if stuck, close OC and delete pending-orders file.

Strategy Patterns & Worked Examples

Catalog of systematic archetypes — structure and edge, not finished systems. Validate on current data before risking capital.

Mean reversion

mr_sample — the canonical reference
MaxSetups:    MaxPositions - S.Positions
SetupScore:   1000 - ROC(C, 5)   // favor weakest
EntryLimit:   L
ExitRule:     BarsHeld >= holdDays
→Short side carries real tail risk — large drawdowns in 1999–2000, 2020, and squeeze events. Use a long MA filter to suppress entries in falling markets.
Connors RSI(2) / PowerZones
EntrySetup: C > MA(C,200) and RSI(2) < 25
ExitRule:   RSI(2) > 70

Works best on liquid index ETFs. March 2020 exposes the tail risk of buying falling knives.

IBS — internal bar strength filter
Data:
    IBS: (C - L) / (H - L)
EntrySetup: ...existing MR setup... and IBS < 0.3

Filter, not standalone edge. Tighter IBS cap raises fill rate but lowers per-trade expectancy — a tradeoff, not a free lunch.

Connors TPS — scaling into weakness with multiple legs
Strategy: L1
    EntrySetup: trendFilter and RSI(2) < 25
Strategy: L2
    EntrySetup: trendFilter and Extern(@L1, BarsHeld > 0 and C < FillPrice)

Strong on indices (SPY/QQQ/DIA), weak on individual stocks. Requires MaxExposure cap.

Trend following & breakout

Donchian breakout — ratcheting stop
EntryStop: Highest(H, 20) + TickSize
ExitStop:  max(PrevExitStop, Lowest(L, 10) - TickSize)
→Forum consensus: MAR ≥ 1 from pure trend following on a broad stock universe is very hard. Lives better on futures, small-caps, or rotational momentum.
Trend following vs breakout — the design distinction

Trend following: enters on breakout, exits only on trailing stop — rides the whole trend. Breakout: same entry, adds profit target / time stop / indicator exit — captures a shorter directional move. Same entry, different exit philosophy, very different holding periods and statistics.

Stockbee momentum burst — cautionary note on mechanization
⚠Purely mechanical version deteriorated badly after mid-2000s. The live trader's judgment about which setups to take is doing much of the work. Before mechanizing a discretionary system, search whether others already tried — the forum is an honest record of what was attempted and what failed.

Momentum rotation & asset allocation

Dual momentum (GEM) — Antonacci
Data:
    SPYROC:    Extern($SPY, ROC(C,12))
    HoldWhat:  if(SPYROC > VEUROC, if(SPYROC > BILROC, $SPY, $AGG),
                  if(VEUROC > BILROC, $VEU, $AGG))
Strategy:
    EntrySetup: HoldWhat = Symbol
    ExitRule:   HoldWhat != Symbol or Dividend > 0
Keller canary allocation (HAA/BAA)
Data:
    factor:  1/4 * (12*ROC(C,1) + 4*ROC(C,3) + 2*ROC(C,6) + 1*ROC(C,12))
    offRank: #rank if(InList("Offensive"), factor, NaN)

Canary assets act as market-health tripwire. Strong in crises, lags in narrow-leadership markets. Example scripts ship with RealTest.

Cascading multi-rank rotation
Data:
    rank1: #rank canHold * ROC(C,500)
    rank2: #rank (rank1 <= cut1) * ROC(C,250)
    rank3: #rank (rank2 <= cut2) * ROC(C,125)
EntrySetup: EndOfMonth and rank3 <= positions and bullMarket

Robust across parameter sweeps. Market filter is critical.

Permanent / All-Weather portfolio with DynamicSizing
Template: Common
    DynamicSizing: True
    QtyType: Percent
Strategy: _SPY
    Quantity: Select(EndOfMonth and Symbol = $SPY, 30)

Appeal: no optimizable parameters, rebalances in place without round-trips.

Bensdorp multi-system framework

The meta-lesson

The lesson is the combination, not any single piece: long trend + long MR + short MR + volatility variants on one shared pool so each system's weak period is covered by another.

→Documented edge decay since ~2019 — published-era returns exceed recent backtests. Validate on current data before trading. The full bensdorp_book.rts is in the Examples folder.
Bensdorp NDX — cautionary split-adjustment error

Ranking on C*V*split instead of C*V introduced look-ahead bias that inflated results. Always check that ranking inputs are unadjusted.

Hedging leg finding
⚠The book's index-short hedge is a consistent money-loser as standalone. A "hedge" has a cost that must be justified by what it protects — don't assume a hedge adds value without testing it independently.

MOC & day trading

Radge MOC day trade — reference structure
EntrySetup: InRUI and C > 20
SetupScore: 1000 - roc5
EntryLimit: L - atr5
ExitRule:   1
ExitTime:   AtClose

Add LimitExtra to reflect fill uncertainty near the low. Rank by recent setup quality, not past symbol performance.

→ThisClose entry cannot be a live order — use NextClose-based logic for live trading.

Overlays, hedges & combining systems

Market breadth regime filter
Data:
    breadth: Extern($#SPX%MA20, C)
EntrySetup: breadth <= breadthValue and Universe
Pairs / relative value
Data:
    ratio: Extern($NVDA, C) / Extern($AAPL, C)
    z:     (ratio - Avg(ratio, 20)) / StdDev(ratio, 20)

Two legs = two strategies. Must be cointegrated, not just correlated. Sizing sync is non-trivial.

Combining non-correlated systems — the meta-pattern
Template: Common
    Allocation: Combined(S.Equity)
Strategy: trend
Strategy: meanrev
Strategy: shortleg
→Combined pool result differs from summing standalone backtests. Capital contention and same-symbol overlap are real interaction effects — always test the combination, not the parts in isolation. Add Combined(Shares) = 0 to EntrySetup of each strategy to prevent cross-strategy overlap.
When a pattern resists mechanization

Some celebrated chart patterns (Minervini VCP, Stockbee burst) have repeatedly defeated full mechanization on the forum. The recurring finding: their edge depends on context and selection judgment that's hard to reduce to rules. Search the forum for prior attempts before committing to replicate a discretionary system.
